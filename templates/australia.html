Top Australian PR Pathways

We specialize in identifying the best visa subclass for your specific occupation and experience level. Here are the most popular routes to Australian Permanent Residency:

1. Skilled Independent Visa (Subclass 189)

The most sought-after PR visa. It does not require a sponsor or state nomination. If your occupation is on the MLTSSL (Medium and Long-term Strategic Skills List) and you score enough points, you can live and work anywhere in Australia.

2. Skilled Nominated Visa (Subclass 190)

If your occupation is in demand in a specific Australian state or territory, they can nominate you. This grants you an extra 5 PR points and allows you to settle in that specific region.

3. Employer Sponsored Visas (TSS 482 / 186 PR)

For professionals who have a concrete job offer from an approved Australian employer. This can act as a direct route to PR or a transition pathway after working in Australia for a few years.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum points requirement for Australia PR?

You must score a minimum of 65 points on the SkillSelect system to lodge an Expression of Interest (EOI). Points are awarded for age, English proficiency, work experience, and education.

What is a Skills Assessment?

Before you can apply for PR, an authorized Australian assessing body must verify that your education and work experience meet the Australian standards for your nominated occupation.

Do I need a job offer from an Australian company?

No! If you are applying under the General Skilled Migration programs (Subclass 189 or 190), you do not need a job offer to secure your Permanent Residency.
