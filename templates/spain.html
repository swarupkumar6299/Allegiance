Top Spanish Immigration Pathways

Whether you are working for a foreign company or have a local job offer, we help you navigate the Spanish Ministry of Inclusion's requirements:

1. The Digital Nomad Visa (DNV)

Perfect for remote workers and freelancers whose clients or employers are outside of Spain. It grants up to a 3-year residence permit, allows you to bring your family, and offers a highly favorable flat tax rate (under the "Beckham Law").

2. Highly Qualified Professional (HQP) Visa

Designed for non-EU citizens who have a local job offer in Spain for a managerial or highly technical role. This fast-tracked visa is processed through the Large Companies Unit (UGE) in just 20 days.

3. Intra-Company Transfer Visa

For employees of multinational companies who are being transferred to a Spanish branch or subsidiary, offering streamlined entry and family inclusion.

The Process

Your Journey to Spain

Spanish immigration involves specific paperwork, but our experts guide you seamlessly from application to arrival.

01

NIE Assignment

We help you obtain your Foreigner Identity Number (NIE), which is required for all legal and financial transactions in Spain.

02

Document Prep

We assist with gathering, translating (via sworn translators), and apostilling your criminal records, degrees, and work contracts.

03

UGE Submission

We submit your application directly to the Large Companies and Strategic Collectives Unit (UGE) for expedited processing.

04

TIE Collection

After approval and arrival in Spain, we guide you through fingerprinting and collecting your physical TIE (Foreigner Identity Card).

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the income requirement for the Digital Nomad Visa?

Currently, the primary applicant must prove an income of at least 200% of the Spanish minimum wage (SMI), which translates to roughly €2,646 per month or €31,752 per year. This amount increases if you bring family members.

What is the "Beckham Law"?

It is a special tax regime that allows foreigners moving to Spain (including Digital Nomads and HQPs) to pay a flat tax rate of 24% on their Spanish-sourced income up to €600,000, rather than the progressive tax rate which can reach up to 47%.

Do I need to speak Spanish?

No, you do not need to pass a Spanish language test to secure the Digital Nomad Visa or the HQP visa. However, learning Spanish is highly recommended for daily life and is required if you eventually apply for citizenship.
