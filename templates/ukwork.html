Points-Based Immigration

Why Apply for a UK Skilled Worker Visa?

The United Kingdom is a global hub for business, finance, healthcare, and technology. Under the new Points-Based Immigration System, skilled professionals with a valid job offer from a UK-approved employer can fast-track their move to the UK and enjoy a high standard of living.

The 70-Point Requirement

To qualify for a Skilled Worker Visa, you must score a total of 70 points. We help you navigate these requirements seamlessly:

1. Valid Job Offer & CoS (50 Points)

You automatically get 50 mandatory points if you have a job offer from an approved UK sponsor at the required skill level, and you can speak English to the required standard.

2. Salary Threshold (20 Points)

The remaining 20 "tradable" points are typically earned if your job pays the required minimum salary, or if your occupation is on the Immigration Salary List (e.g., healthcare, engineering).

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)?

A CoS is an electronic record issued by your UK employer proving you have been offered a legitimate job. You must have the unique CoS reference number to apply for the Skilled Worker visa.

Do I need to take an English test?

Yes, unless you are from an exempt country or hold a degree taught in English. You must prove your knowledge of English by passing an approved Secure English Language Test (SELT) at level B1 or higher.

How long does the Skilled Worker Visa last?

Your visa can last for up to 5 years before it needs to be extended. After 5 continuous years of living and working in the UK, you become eligible to apply to settle permanently (Indefinite Leave to Remain).
