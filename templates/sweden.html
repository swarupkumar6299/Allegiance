Top Swedish Residency Pathways

We specialize in navigating the requirements set by Migrationsverket (the Swedish Migration Agency) to ensure your application is processed smoothly:

1. Swedish Work Permit (Arbetstillstånd)

The standard route for professionals with a valid job offer from a Swedish employer. The position must offer a salary that meets the newly updated maintenance requirements and terms that match Swedish collective agreements.

2. Job Seeker Visa for Highly Educated

A newly introduced 9-month visa for individuals with an advanced degree (Master's or PhD). It allows you to move to Sweden to look for work or explore starting your own business without needing a prior job offer.

3. The EU Blue Card (Sweden)

For highly qualified workers with a university degree and an employment contract offering a highly competitive salary (at least 1.5 times the average gross salary in Sweden). Offers enhanced mobility within the EU.

The Process

Your Journey to Sweden

Swedish immigration requires strict compliance with labor union standards. Our experts manage the bureaucracy so you can focus on relocating.

01

Contract & Union Approval

We ensure your job offer meets the strict salary thresholds and that your employer has obtained the required trade union approval.

02

Migrationsverket Filing

We file your application with the Swedish Migration Agency, prioritizing fast-track processing wherever eligible.

03

Biometrics & UT Card

You will visit the Swedish embassy to submit biometrics. Upon approval, your UT Card (Residence Permit Card) is issued for travel.

04

Skatteverket Registration

Upon arrival, we guide you through registering with the Tax Agency (Skatteverket) to obtain your vital Personnummer and BankID.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to speak Swedish to work there?

For most tech, engineering, and international corporate roles, the working language is 100% English, and Swedes are among the best non-native English speakers in the world. However, learning Swedish is highly recommended for long-term integration and everyday society.

What is a Personnummer and BankID?

The Personnummer is your Swedish personal identity number, issued by Skatteverket. It is required for everything: healthcare, paying taxes, opening a bank account, and getting a gym membership. BankID is your digital signature linked to your bank, which is used for all secure online logins in Sweden.

Can my unmarried partner move with me?

Yes! Swedish immigration law is very progressive. If you can prove you have lived together in a serious relationship (known as being a "sambo"), your partner can be included in your application and will receive full work rights.
