Top Portuguese Residency Pathways

We specialize in identifying the fastest and most reliable route to your Portuguese residency. Our experts handle the exact requirements for each specific "D" visa subclass:

1. D8 Digital Nomad Visa

The newest and most popular visa for remote workers and freelancers. If you earn at least 4x the Portuguese minimum wage from clients or an employer outside of Portugal, you can secure a 2-year residency permit that is renewable.

2. D7 Passive Income Visa

Designed for retirees or independent wealth earners. If you receive regular passive income (such as pensions, real estate rentals, dividends, or investments) that meets the minimum wage requirement, this is your fastest route to residency.

3. D3 Highly Qualified Professional Visa

For highly skilled professionals (IT, engineering, medicine, management) who have secured an employment contract or a binding job offer from a company based in Portugal. This acts as a fast-track to the EU Blue Card.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum income for the D8 Digital Nomad Visa?

The primary applicant must prove a monthly income equal to four times the Portuguese minimum wage. Currently, this translates to approximately €3,280 per month. You must also show savings in a Portuguese bank account.

What is an NIF and why do I need it?

The NIF (Número de Identificação Fiscal) is your Portuguese tax identification number. It is mandatory for virtually everything in Portugal, including signing a lease, opening a bank account, setting up utilities, and applying for your visa.

How long does it take to get Portuguese Citizenship?

Portugal has one of the most favorable citizenship timelines in Europe. You are eligible to apply for citizenship or permanent residency after holding legal residency status for 5 continuous years. You will need to pass a basic A2 level Portuguese language test at that time.
